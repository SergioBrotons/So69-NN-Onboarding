## Section 1 — Strategic Purpose

SIM-04 ensures personnel can participate effectively in cross-role workshops—contributing specialized knowledge while integrating input from colleagues with different expertise, perspectives, and priorities. This simulation develops the collaborative intelligence required to solve complex problems that no single role can address alone.

## Section 3 — Core Explanation

### Concept Definition

Cross-role workshops bring together personnel from different functional areas—sales, product, technical, compliance, and data teams—to collectively solve problems or develop solutions. Effective participation requires balancing contribution of specialized expertise with integration of other perspectives, managing disagreements constructively, and maintaining focus on collective objectives.

### Why This Matters

Complex business challenges require diverse expertise. Sales understands customer needs, technical understands feasibility, compliance understands constraints, and data understands measurement. No single perspective is sufficient. The organization's ability to develop comprehensive solutions depends on personnel who can collaborate across role boundaries.

### The Discipline Requirement

Cross-role collaboration demands intellectual humility, active listening, and translation skills. Specialists must communicate in ways that others can understand, while remaining open to valid input from different perspectives. The discipline is not merely contributing knowledge but synthesizing a collective capability that exceeds any individual contribution.

---

## Section 4 — Scenario

### The Setting

A cross-functional workshop has been convened to address a significant business challenge: optimizing the customer journey while maintaining compliance and technical feasibility. Participants include representatives from sales, product, technical, compliance, and data functions. The facilitator guides the group through structured exercises requiring integrated input from all perspectives.

### The Challenge

The simulation places personnel in situations requiring:

- Explaining technical constraints to non-technical colleagues
- Translating business requirements into feasible technical solutions
- Identifying compliance implications that other roles may overlook
- Synthesizing conflicting priorities into coherent recommendations
- Making trade-off decisions with incomplete information
- Maintaining productive collaboration when disagreements arise

### Workshop Structure

The simulation includes multiple phases:

- Individual preparation and perspective articulation
- Group problem framing and scope definition
- Role-specific input sessions with Q&A
- Integration exercises combining perspectives
- Trade-off analysis and decision-making
- Solution presentation and defense

## Section 5 — Assessment Rubric

The simulation evaluates multiple dimensions of cross-role collaboration:

### Contribution Quality (20 points)

Specialized knowledge must be accurately communicated, relevant to the problem, and useful for collective solution development. Contributions should add distinctive value that only the individual's role perspective can provide.

### Integration Capability (20 points)

Personnel must demonstrate ability to incorporate input from other roles, build on colleagues' contributions, and synthesize diverse perspectives into coherent analysis. Working in isolation despite being in a collaborative setting indicates failure to engage.

### Communication Clarity (20 points)

Technical concepts must be translated for non-technical audiences. Compliance constraints must be explained in business terms. Data insights must be connected to actionable implications. Clarity enables collective progress.

### Collaboration Posture (20 points)

Intellectual humility, openness to alternative perspectives, and constructive engagement with disagreement indicate mature collaboration capability. Defensive posturing, dismissiveness, or insistence on one's own view without merit undermines collective intelligence.

### Outcome Focus (20 points)

Workshops exist to produce results. Personnel should maintain focus on deliverables, manage time effectively, and drive toward conclusions rather than allowing endless discussion without resolution.

**Passing**: 70/100 points with no single dimension below 10 points

## Section 6 — Failure Modes

### Common Failure Patterns

Personnel frequently fail this simulation through predictable patterns that reveal inability to work collaboratively across role boundaries.

**Specialized Siloing**: Contributing only from one's own perspective without engaging with others, or dismissing input from other roles as irrelevant, demonstrates failure to understand interdisciplinary collaboration.

**Translation Failure**: Using jargon specific to one's own role without explanation, or failing to connect specialized input to the broader problem, creates barriers to integration.

**Defensive Rigidness**: Insisting on one's own position without considering merit in alternative approaches, or treating criticism as attack rather than input,poison collaboration.

**Passive Non-Participation**: Remaining silent when input is needed, failing to challenge questionable assumptions, or waiting for others to drive progress indicates lack of ownership.

**Outcome Avoidance**: Prioritizing discussion over decisions, avoiding trade-off decisions, or failing to commit to recommendations undermines workshop effectiveness.

## Section 8 — Preparation Protocol

### Pre-Simulation Requirements

Before attempting this simulation, personnel must complete:

- Full comprehension of CORE-01 content
- Understanding of all other role perspectives in the organization
- Review of the specific business challenge being addressed
- Preparation of role-specific input and constraints

### Recommended Preparation Activities

**Perspective Mapping**: For each role likely to be represented, document what they care about, what constraints they face, and what language they use. This preparation enables effective translation during the workshop.

**Challenge Anticipation**: Consider the likely business problem and prepare specific input from your role perspective. Identify constraints, opportunities, and trade-offs relevant to your function.

**Collaboration Practice**: Work with colleagues from other roles on smaller problems to develop comfort with cross-functional interaction before high-stakes workshops.

**Active Listening Exercises**: Practice the discipline of truly hearing other perspectives before formulating responses. Many collaboration failures stem from listening to respond rather than listening to understand.
